Your Guide to Growing Organic Mushrooms from Farm Waste

This guide will show you how to turn farm waste into a sustainable food resource by growing organic mushrooms.


Farm waste can feed more than just livestock...

It can be the foundation for a thriving mushroom farm right in your backyard.

The process:

Select a substrate readily available on your farm. Substrates act as food for mycelium and a home for fungi.

Shred or chop the substrate into manageable 3-4 inch pieces. The smaller, the better.

Clean the substrate thoroughly to remove any contaminants and pasteurize the it by boiling it for at least one hour to ensure it's sterile.

Let the substrate cool down to the right moisture level. Squeeze it; no water should drip, but your hand should feel damp.

Add mushroom spawn (seed material) to the pasteurized substrate. Make proper holes covered with cotton to allow airflow as mycelium releases a lot of CO2.

Incubate the substrate under proper environmental conditions. The bag should turn completely white during incubation.

After 10-12 days, once incubation is complete, maintain humidity and fresh air flow, and harvest your mushrooms!

Estimated Time 10-12 days for incubation and growth.

Why Grow Mushrooms?

Not only are mushrooms a high-value product, but they also offer a way to recycle farm waste efficiently. It's a sustainable, profitable venture! The best thing is you can make it at the comfort of your homeüòä
