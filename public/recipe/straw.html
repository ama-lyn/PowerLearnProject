Turning Straw into High-Quality Animal Feed

Straw to Animal Feed

Straw is often overlooked on farms, but with the right approach, it can be transformed into nutritious animal feed. This recipe shows you how to maximize the potential of straw.


Did you know? Straw is rich in fiber, which helps improve digestion for animals.

The process:

Collect dry straw. You'll need about 10 kg of straw to start. Make sure the straw is clean and free from any contaminants like plastic or large debris.

Chop the straw into smaller pieces. You can use a straw chopper or simply cut it manually. The smaller the pieces, the easier it is for animals to digest.

Soak the chopped straw in a nutrient solution (made from molasses, water, and a small amount of urea) for 24 hours. This will increase its nutritional value and make it more palatable for livestock.

After soaking, drain the excess solution and spread the straw out to dry for 1-2 days, turning it over occasionally to prevent mold.

Once dried, the straw is now ready to be fed to your livestock or stored for later use. You can blend it with other feed materials for a balanced diet.

Estimated Time 3 days for the full process.

You may be wondering, What are the benefits? ü§î

This method improves the digestibility of straw and enhances its nutritional value, making it a cost-effective alternative to expensive commercial feed. Plus, it helps reduce farm waste!

Materials Needed

10 kg of straw
Nutrient solution (Molasses, Water, Urea)
Straw chopper
Large container for soaking
Drying area
Storage bags (optional)
