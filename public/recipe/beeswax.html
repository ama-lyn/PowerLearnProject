In this recipe, we will focus on transforming beeswax, a natural byproduct of beekeeping, into beautiful and fragrant candles that enhance your space.


Beeswax candles are not only environmentally friendly...

They also emit a warm glow and subtle honey aroma, perfect for creating a cozy atmosphere.

The process:

Gather your materials: beeswax, candle wicks, and a double boiler or microwave-safe container and any desired essential oils for fragrance.

Melt the beeswax in the double boiler over low heat while stirring occasionally or in short bursts in the microwave until fully liquid.

If desired, add a few drops of essential oil to the melted wax for fragrance. Mix thoroughly.

Secure the wick in the center of your mold and slowly pour the melted beeswax into the mold, ensuring the wick stays centered.

Allow the candle to cool completely before removing it from the mold. Trim the wick to about 1/4 inch before lighting.

Estimated Time Approximately 1-2 hours for making and cooling the candles.

Beeswax candles burn longer and cleaner than paraffin and regular candles, are non-toxic, and help purify the air, making them a great addition to any home. Try making it own your own!

Materials Needed

Pure beeswax
Candle wicks
Double boiler or (microwave-safe tin)
Essential oils (optional)
Candle holders
